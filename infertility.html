Infertility Evaluation & Treatment

What is it?

Infertility affects 1 in 6 couples. If you have been trying to conceive for 12 months (or 6 months if over 35) without success, a thorough evaluation helps pinpoint the cause and chart the most effective treatment path.

Who needs it?

Unexplained Infertility PCOS Male Factor Infertility Recurrent Pregnancy Loss Hormonal Imbalance Ovulation Disorders Tubal Factor

What to expect

  1. 1
    Comprehensive History & Examination

    Detailed medical, menstrual and reproductive history for both partners — identifying risk factors, prior investigations, and lifestyle contributors.

  2. 2
    Targeted Investigations

    Blood hormone panel (FSH, LH, AMH, thyroid), transvaginal ultrasound, semen analysis, and hysteroscopy or laparoscopy if indicated.

  3. 3
    Personalised Treatment Plan

    Medical, surgical, or assisted reproductive technology (ART) pathway — tailored to the findings and the couple's specific goals and circumstances.

Intra-Uterine Insemination (IUI)

What is it?

IUI is a fertility procedure where specially prepared sperm are placed directly into the uterus around the time of ovulation, improving the chances of fertilisation. It is the least invasive assisted reproductive technique — often the first step before moving to IVF.

Who is it for?

Unexplained Infertility Mild Male Factor Cervical Mucus Issues Donor Sperm Ovulatory Dysfunction

What to expect

  1. 1
    Ovulation Monitoring

    Ultrasound tracking of follicle growth; a trigger injection is given when the dominant follicle reaches 18–20 mm to time the procedure precisely.

  2. 2
    Semen Preparation

    The sperm sample is washed and concentrated in the laboratory on the day of the procedure, removing inactive sperm and seminal fluid.

  3. 3
    Insemination & Result

    A fine catheter places prepared sperm directly into the uterus — a painless procedure taking 5–10 minutes. Pregnancy test is done 14 days later.

IVF-ET (In Vitro Fertilisation & Embryo Transfer)

What is it?

IVF is the most effective form of assisted reproductive technology. Eggs are retrieved from the ovaries, fertilised with sperm in a laboratory, and the resulting embryo is transferred into the uterus. Recommended when simpler treatments have not succeeded.

Who needs it?

Blocked Fallopian Tubes Severe Male Factor Failed IUI Cycles Poor Ovarian Reserve Endometriosis Genetic Screening (PGT) Advanced Maternal Age

What to expect

  1. 1
    Ovarian Stimulation

    10–14 days of injectable hormones encourage multiple follicles to develop. Close monitoring via ultrasound and blood tests ensures optimal response.

  2. 2
    Egg Retrieval & Fertilisation

    Eggs are collected transvaginally under sedation. In the laboratory, eggs are fertilised with prepared sperm and embryos are cultured for 3–5 days.

  3. 3
    Embryo Transfer

    The best-quality embryo(s) are placed into the uterus via a fine catheter — a simple, usually painless procedure. Pregnancy test follows after 14 days. Surplus embryos may be frozen for future use.

Female Infertility Treatment

What is it?

Many causes of female infertility — PCOS, thyroid dysfunction, endometriosis, uterine abnormalities — respond excellently to targeted medical or minimally invasive surgical treatment, often restoring natural conception without the need for ART.

Conditions treated

PCOS Endometriosis Thyroid Disorders Uterine Fibroids & Polyps Premature Ovarian Insufficiency Recurrent Implantation Failure Luteal Phase Defect

What to expect

  1. 1
    Diagnosis

    Hormonal workup, transvaginal ultrasound, and diagnostic laparoscopy or hysteroscopy where indicated to precisely identify the underlying cause.

  2. 2
    Targeted Treatment

    PCOS: ovulation induction + lifestyle modification. Endometriosis: laparoscopic excision. Uterine polyps/fibroids: hysteroscopic correction. Thyroid: hormone optimisation.

  3. 3
    Conception Support

    Timed intercourse guidance, luteal phase support, and early pregnancy monitoring — ensuring the best chance of a successful, sustained pregnancy.
