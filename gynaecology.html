Laparoscopy

What is it?

Laparoscopy is a minimally invasive surgical technique using a thin, telescope-like instrument (laparoscope) inserted through tiny incisions in the abdomen. It allows Dr. Nupura to both diagnose and treat pelvic conditions with precision — eliminating the need for large open-surgery incisions, reducing recovery time, and leaving minimal scarring.

Who needs it?

Endometriosis Ovarian Cysts Uterine Fibroids Unexplained Pelvic Pain Blocked Fallopian Tubes Ectopic Pregnancy Infertility Investigation Adhesions (Scar Tissue)

What to expect

  1. 1
    Consultation & Investigation

    Detailed clinical history, pelvic examination, blood tests, and an ultrasound to plan the procedure.

  2. 2
    Procedure

    Performed under general anaesthesia. 2–3 small incisions are made; CO₂ gas is used to create space. Duration is typically 30–90 minutes depending on findings.

  3. 3
    Recovery

    Same-day or next-day discharge in most cases. Mild soreness for a few days. Most patients return to normal activities within 1–2 weeks.

Hysterectomy (Abdominal & Vaginal)

What is it?

Hysterectomy is the surgical removal of the uterus. An abdominal hysterectomy is performed through an incision in the lower abdomen, while a vaginal hysterectomy is done entirely through the vagina with no external incision — leading to faster healing and no visible scar. Dr. Nupura tailors the approach to each patient's anatomy and condition.

Who needs it?

Uterine Fibroids Endometriosis Uterine Prolapse Abnormal Uterine Bleeding Chronic Pelvic Pain Adenomyosis Gynaecological Cancer (early stage)

What to expect

  1. 1
    Preoperative Assessment

    Blood tests, imaging, anaesthesia evaluation, and a detailed discussion of surgical options (abdominal vs. vaginal) and implications.

  2. 2
    Surgery

    Performed under general or spinal anaesthesia. Duration is typically 1–2 hours. The uterus (and sometimes ovaries/fallopian tubes) is removed depending on the indication.

  3. 3
    Recovery

    Hospital stay of 2–4 days. Full recovery takes 4–6 weeks. Vaginal hysterectomy generally has a faster return to activity.

Tubectomy & Tubal Ligation

What is it?

Tubectomy (female sterilisation) is a permanent method of contraception in which the fallopian tubes are cut, tied, or sealed — preventing eggs from reaching the uterus and sperm from fertilising them. It is performed laparoscopically, making it minimally invasive with a very quick recovery.

Who needs it?

Permanent Contraception Family Complete Medical Contraindication to Pregnancy Postpartum Sterilisation

What to expect

  1. 1
    Counselling & Consent

    A thorough discussion of the permanent nature of the procedure, alternatives, and the patient's decision. No pressure — your choice, your timeline.

  2. 2
    Laparoscopic Procedure

    Performed under general anaesthesia through 2 small incisions. Takes approximately 20–30 minutes. Clips or rings are used to seal the tubes.

  3. 3
    Recovery

    Same-day discharge. Mild discomfort for 1–2 days. Most women return to routine activities within 3–5 days. Immediately effective.

Copper T Insertion & Removal

What is it?

The Copper T (IUD) is a small, T-shaped intrauterine device placed inside the uterus to prevent pregnancy for up to 10 years. It works by releasing copper ions, which are toxic to sperm. It is hormone-free, reversible, and one of the most effective forms of long-term contraception. Insertion and removal are quick in-clinic procedures — no surgery required.

Who needs it?

Long-Term Non-Hormonal Contraception Hormone-Sensitive Conditions Postpartum Contraception Emergency Contraception (within 5 days) Breastfeeding Mothers

What to expect

  1. 1
    Brief Pelvic Examination

    Dr. Nupura checks the position and size of the uterus to ensure safe placement. A pap smear or STI screen may be recommended.

  2. 2
    Insertion

    A quick in-clinic procedure lasting 5–10 minutes. A speculum is used and the IUD is threaded into the uterine cavity. Mild cramping is normal during insertion.

  3. 3
    Follow-up

    A check-up at 6 weeks to confirm correct placement. The device is effective immediately and can be removed any time if pregnancy is desired.
