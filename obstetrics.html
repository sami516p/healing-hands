Obstetrics

Compassionate pregnancy care from your first consultation through delivery — and every milestone in between.

In this page Maternal Care & Checkup Normal Vaginal Delivery High-Risk Pregnancy Antenatal Monitoring

Maternal Care & Checkup

What is it?

Comprehensive antenatal care throughout pregnancy — tracking the health of both mother and baby through regular clinical checkups, blood investigations, and ultrasound monitoring. Dr. Nupura's approach is personalised, unhurried, and centred on clear communication at every stage. Starting early gives the best outcomes for mother and child.

Who needs it?

All Pregnant Women First-Time Mothers Planned Pregnancies Previous Pregnancy Loss Pre-Existing Medical Conditions

What to expect

  1. 1
    First Trimester (0–12 weeks)

    Confirmation of pregnancy, dating scan, blood group, haemoglobin, thyroid, and early anomaly screen. Monthly visits.

  2. 2
    Second Trimester (13–28 weeks)

    Anomaly scan at 18–20 weeks, glucose screening, fetal growth assessment, blood pressure and weight monitoring. Fortnightly visits.

  3. 3
    Third Trimester (28–40 weeks)

    Weekly visits, growth scans, non-stress tests, fetal position assessment, and birth planning discussion.

Normal Vaginal Delivery (NVD)

What is it?

Natural childbirth through the vaginal canal, guided and managed by Dr. Nupura throughout the entire labour and delivery process. NVD is the preferred mode of delivery when there are no medical contraindications — it carries the fastest maternal recovery, supports breastfeeding, and benefits the baby's gut microbiome and immune development.

Who is it suitable for?

Singleton Pregnancy (one baby) Head-Down (Cephalic) Position No Placenta Previa No Active Medical Contraindication VBAC (Vaginal Birth After Caesarean)

What to expect

  1. 1
    Labour Monitoring

    Regular assessment of cervical dilation, fetal heart rate monitoring (CTG), and maternal vitals during active labour.

  2. 2
    Delivery

    Dr. Nupura guides the pushing phase and delivery of the baby and placenta. Pain relief options (epidural, gas) are discussed beforehand based on preference.

  3. 3
    Post-Delivery Care

    Immediate skin-to-skin contact and breastfeeding initiation. Mother is monitored for 2 hours post-delivery. Hospital stay typically 24–48 hours.

High-Risk Pregnancy Care

What is it?

Specialised monitoring and management for pregnancies with elevated risk — either due to the mother's health, fetal factors, or complications arising during pregnancy. Dr. Nupura coordinates closely with specialists (cardiologist, nephrologist, endocrinologist) when needed, ensuring the safest possible outcome for both mother and baby.

Who needs it?

Gestational Diabetes Preeclampsia / Hypertension Twin or Multiple Pregnancy Advanced Maternal Age (35+) Previous Caesarean Thyroid Disorders Placenta Previa Previous Preterm Birth Fetal Growth Restriction

What to expect

  1. 1
    Risk Stratification

    Early identification of risk factors, comprehensive investigations, and a tailored antenatal care plan — with more frequent visits than a routine pregnancy.

  2. 2
    Intensive Monitoring

    Additional Doppler ultrasounds, growth scans every 2–4 weeks, non-stress tests, and close blood pressure and blood sugar tracking.

  3. 3
    Delivery Planning

    Timing and mode of delivery are carefully planned based on the specific risk. Dr. Nupura ensures the hospital team is briefed and prepared for any eventuality.

Antenatal Monitoring

What is it?

Systematic, structured tracking of fetal growth, position, and wellbeing throughout all three trimesters. Antenatal monitoring uses a combination of clinical examination, ultrasound scanning, and electronic fetal monitoring (non-stress tests) to detect any issues early and intervene before they become serious.

Key tests & scans

Dating Scan (6–8 weeks) NT Scan (11–14 weeks) Anomaly Scan (18–20 weeks) Growth Scan (28–36 weeks) Non-Stress Test (NST) Doppler Velocimetry Fetal Kick Count Biophysical Profile (BPP)

What to expect

  1. 1
    First & Second Trimester Scans

    Dating, viability, nuchal translucency, and the detailed anomaly scan — checking all fetal organs and structures for normal development.

  2. 2
    Third Trimester Monitoring

    Serial growth scans to track fetal weight and position. NST to assess fetal heart rate patterns. Doppler studies to check placental blood flow.

  3. 3
    Ongoing Guidance

    Dr. Nupura explains each finding in plain language at every visit — so you always know exactly how your pregnancy is progressing.
